About

Welcome to the TJHSST Microelectronics Research Lab, affectionately known as "the Lab"! Here, some of TJ's finest and brighest minds come together to study anything and everything in the field of electronics. No matter what you're interested in, there's always something for you in the Lab.

We're led by Mrs. Pamela Ahn, and we're located in Curie Commons on the second floor, across the hall from the Automation and Robotics Lab (Robo).

Electronics Club

Compared to the freshman experience in Design and Tech, Electronics Club takes a much deeper look at the network of signals that powers everything around us, giving you a foundational perspective critical to understanding the modern digitized age. Here you can learn everything that’s covered in the electronics electives (Analog, Digital, Microprocessors, and Audio Electronics) and much more! Culminating in a final individual project, members get to learn how to process electronic signals to play audio on a speaker, create their own games, and many more exciting uses of electronics.

Meetings will be conducted by seniors in the Lab, who will organize lectures and help members in both building simulations of circuits and learning the factors involved in building real ones. You’ll also be able to talk with members of the Lab about anything you might be curious about regarding TJ’s electronics offerings (and much more!). Members of all experience levels are welcome, from freshmen who haven’t even heard of the robot project (be thankful you haven’t yet) to students in the middle of taking the electronics electives! Club meetings will take place on Fridays A and B block.
